# v0.44 Safe And Shop Supply Loop Design

## Goal

Make SafeRoom and ShopRoom feel like real roguelike support nodes instead of passive room labels. SafeRoom gives a readable recovery beat. ShopRoom spends a lightweight in-run supply currency on one reward choice, with clear feedback when the player cannot afford it.

## Scope

- SafeRoom remains enemy-free and immediately returns to route choice after applying recovery.
- Battle and Elite clears grant in-run supply credits.
- ShopRoom opens a smaller reward draft, but selecting a shop reward costs supply credits.
- If the player cannot afford a shop reward, the shop stays open and shows a readable "not enough supplies" message.
- Room choice previews and room feedback show the current supply count, shop cost, and SafeRoom recovery value.
- AI companion speech reacts to entering SafeRoom, entering ShopRoom, buying a supply reward, and being short on supplies.

## Architecture

- `RunSupplyRules` is a small pure rules class for supply gains, shop cost, and readable labels.
- `RunManager` owns the current run supply count and shop feedback state because route, reward, and room UI already read from it.
- `RoomRewardChoiceUI` adds cost/affordability text while continuing to use the existing reward-choice flow.
- Existing `CompanionSpeechBubbleUI` is reused for AI feedback.

## Player Flow

1. Clear a BattleRoom or EliteRoom.
2. Gain supply credits and see that gain in the room feedback panel.
3. Choose a ShopRoom from the route portal.
4. Shop reward UI shows current supplies and each option cost.
5. Selecting an affordable option spends supplies, applies the reward, and returns to route choice.
6. Selecting an unaffordable option keeps the shop open and shows a clear warning.

## Non-Goals

- No persistent economy.
- No item inventory.
- No reroll, sell, or multi-purchase shop.
- No event-room narrative expansion.

## Verification

- EditMode tests cover rules, gains, spending, unaffordable selection, previews, and feedback text.
- Unity Play smoke verifies HomeScene and SampleScene do not emit errors or warnings.
