# v0.31 Enemy Attack Pattern Design

## Goal

Turn the existing Melee, Ranged, and Guard stat profiles into visibly different, dodgeable combat behaviors without changing Boss attacks.

## Behavior

- Melee starts a short forward lunge when its warning begins. The lunge has a visible red motion marker and remains bounded so it cannot cross the whole room.
- Ranged finishes its warning by launching a visible straight projectile. Damage occurs only when the projectile intersects the moving player, so stepping out of its path avoids damage.
- Guard reduces damage arriving from its facing direction. After committing an attack it enters a brief vulnerable state, changes its shield marker, and takes increased damage from either player or companion.

## Architecture

- `EnemyAttack2D` keeps warning/cooldown ownership and selects direct or projectile delivery.
- `EnemyProjectile2D` owns straight-line movement, segment hit detection, lifetime, damage, and visual state.
- `EnemyAttackPattern2D` owns archetype-specific lunge and Guard defense/vulnerability behavior and implements `IDamageModifier`.
- `RoomManager` configures these components from `EnemyArchetypeType`; existing scenes and prefabs need no serialized migration.
- `EnemyController2D` reads the configured `EnemyAttack2D.AttackRange`, fixing ranged enemies that previously stopped outside their controller's old attack range.

## Feedback And Safety

- Existing role colors are reused for lunge, projectile, and shield markers.
- Room feedback explicitly tells the player to dodge projectiles and punish Guard openings.
- Missing archetype configuration falls back to the existing direct attack, preserving tests, prefab behavior, and Boss configuration.

## Verification

- EditMode tests cover lunge movement, delayed projectile damage, projectile dodging, Guard front block/vulnerability, runtime room wiring, and room feedback.
- A Play Mode smoke run spawns a mixed room, observes a moving projectile, confirms a Guard shield marker and vulnerable state, and checks the console.
